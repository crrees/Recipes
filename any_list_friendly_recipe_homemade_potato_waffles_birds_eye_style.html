Homemade Potato Waffles (Birds Eye-style)

Yield: 4–6 waffles
Prep: 15 min
Cook: 25 min
Total: 45 min
Cuisine: British
Category: Side

Ingredients

  • 500 g floury potatoes (Maris Piper or King Edward), peeled
  • 40 g potato starch
  • 1 egg white
  • 1/2 tsp fine salt
  • 1/4 tsp white pepper
  • 1 tbsp rapeseed oil, plus extra for brushing
  • Optional: 1 tbsp milk or melted butter if mixture feels too dry

Method

  1. Boil and dry potatoes: Cut potatoes into chunks. Boil in salted water 12–15 minutes until very tender. Drain well, mash smooth, then spread out on a tray to steam off for 10 minutes until dry.
  2. Make mixture: In a bowl, combine cooled mash, potato starch, salt and pepper. Add egg white and 1 tbsp oil. Mix until it forms a firm dough. If crumbly, add a splash of milk.
  3. Heat and oil iron: Preheat waffle iron to high. Lightly brush both plates with rapeseed oil.
  4. Cook waffles: Portion mixture into the iron. Close and cook 6–8 minutes until golden and crisp. Rotate halfway if your iron flips.
  5. Finish and serve: For extra crispness, transfer waffles to a wire rack and oven-finish at 220 °C for 5 minutes. Serve hot, or cool and freeze. Reheat from frozen in toaster or air fryer.
